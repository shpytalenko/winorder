Collaborative Drafts
Use Case Description
Assumptions:
Users and account owners belong to same
subdomain.
All actors are authenticated before they can create
or modify drafts.
Data must be valid (per the form) before it is saved
to the draft.
Use Case Description:
Collaborative Drafts & Administrator Approval
Allows users to collaboratively create drafts (data that is a
record, but not approved yet)
Makes one account owner the approver (signature approves
records)
Helps with data loss, and avoids un-needed records from
being stored.
Use Case Description:
Collaborative Drafts & Administrator Approval
Features Needed:
Way of notifying account owner when there is a draft
requiring approval (banner, slide out box, pop-up dialogue
box, etc.)
Transitional storage of data as it progresses into becoming a
record. Drafts are updated after focus is lost for more than a
few seconds.
Control panel for account owner to add/remove permissions
from users on subdomain.
Make Distinction Between a Draft and a Record
Draft: Data that is in the process of being turned into a record.
Record: A draft that has been approved by the account owner.
